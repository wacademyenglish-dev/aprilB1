Grammar: Phrasal Verbs

Analyze the use of the verb "Boss around":

"Kids could boss around their parents."

"Stop bossing me around!"

This phrasal verb is separable. Can we put the object in the middle?

RULE UNLOCKED!

You can say "Boss around the kids" OR "Boss the kids around". Both are perfect!

Who bosses you around more: your boss at work or your partner at home? 😉

Grammar: Passive Voice

How does the presenter describe the French tradition?

"In France, it is known as April Fish Day."

When we focus on the FACT and not WHO does it, we use the Passive Voice (Verb To Be + Past Participle).

RULE UNLOCKED!

"It is known as" (É conhecido como). Perfect for talking about traditions and history!

Does your city have a nickname? "It is known as..."
